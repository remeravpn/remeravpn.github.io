TRADES

Our Trades Are...

TOR

Tourism.

Studying tourism involves learning about global cultures, sustainable travel practices, and the hospitality industry. It provides insights into how tourism impacts economies and environments. Students develop skills in customer service, marketing, and event planning, preparing them for dynamic careers in a growing sector. This field opens opportunities to explore different cultures and contribute to sustainable tourism initiatives.

NIT

Netwoking And Internet Technology.

Studying Networking and Internet Technology at Jill Barham School provides you with essential skills for today's digital world. You'll learn about network design, cybersecurity, and cloud computing, equipping you to manage and secure computer networks. This field offers high-demand career opportunities in IT support, network administration, and systems analysis. It's an excellent choice for anyone looking to build a future in technology and innovation!

FBO

Food And Biverage Operations.

Studying Food and Beverage Operations involves learning about the management and service of food and drinks in various settings like restaurants, hotels, and events. This field covers areas such as menu planning, food safety, cost control, and customer service. Students gain practical skills in overseeing kitchen and dining operations, ensuring quality and efficiency. This education prepares them for careers in the hospitality industry, including roles in management, catering, and culinary arts.

ACC

Accounting.

Studying accounting involves learning about financial reporting, auditing, tax regulations, and budget management. It equips students with skills to analyze financial data, prepare financial statements, and ensure compliance with laws and regulations. This field is essential for effective financial management in businesses, government, and nonprofit organizations. Graduates are prepared for careers in accounting, auditing, financial analysis, and consulting, contributing to the financial health and success of their organizations.
